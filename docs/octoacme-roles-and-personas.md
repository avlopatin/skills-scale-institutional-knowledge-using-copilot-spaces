# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## UX Designer

### Role Summary
UX Designers create user-centered designs that ensure products are intuitive, accessible, and delightful. They bridge user needs with technical feasibility through research, prototyping, and iterative design.

### Responsibilities
- Conduct user research and usability testing
- Create wireframes, mockups, and interactive prototypes
- Define user flows and information architecture
- Collaborate with Product Managers on feature requirements
- Work with Developers to ensure design fidelity during implementation
- Establish and maintain design systems and style guides

### Goals
- Deliver intuitive, accessible user experiences
- Reduce user friction and increase product adoption
- Create consistent design patterns across the product
- Validate designs through user feedback and testing

### Typical Communication
- Design reviews with Product and Engineering teams
- User research findings and recommendations
- Prototype demonstrations and design handoff documentation
- Collaboration with Developers during implementation

### Interactions with Core Roles
- **Product Managers**: Align on user problems and feature prioritization; validate designs meet business goals
- **Developers**: Provide design specifications and assets; collaborate on technical feasibility and implementation
- **Project Managers**: Communicate design timelines and dependencies; flag design-related risks
- **QA Lead**: Define expected user experiences for testing scenarios

---

## Business Analyst

### Role Summary
Business Analysts bridge business needs and technical solutions. They gather requirements, analyze processes, and ensure that project deliverables align with organizational objectives and stakeholder expectations.

### Responsibilities
- Elicit and document business requirements from stakeholders
- Analyze current processes and identify improvement opportunities
- Create process flows, data models, and functional specifications
- Facilitate requirements workshops and stakeholder sessions
- Validate that solutions meet business needs and acceptance criteria
- Support change management and user adoption activities

### Goals
- Ensure clear, actionable requirements that drive business value
- Minimize scope gaps and misunderstandings
- Optimize business processes through technology solutions
- Facilitate alignment between business and technical teams

### Typical Communication
- Requirements documentation and functional specifications
- Stakeholder workshops and validation sessions
- Business process diagrams and user stories
- Change impact assessments

### Interactions with Core Roles
- **Product Managers**: Translate product vision into detailed requirements; validate business value
- **Developers**: Clarify functional requirements and edge cases; support technical estimation
- **Project Managers**: Define scope and acceptance criteria; identify business-side risks and dependencies
- **Stakeholder Representative**: Gather and validate requirements; ensure business alignment

---

## DevOps Engineer

### Role Summary
DevOps Engineers enable fast, reliable, and secure software delivery through automation, infrastructure management, and continuous integration/deployment practices. They reduce friction in the development and release process.

### Responsibilities
- Build and maintain CI/CD pipelines
- Manage infrastructure as code and cloud resources
- Implement monitoring, logging, and alerting systems
- Automate deployment and rollback processes
- Ensure security best practices in infrastructure and pipelines
- Support incident response and system reliability

### Goals
- Enable fast, frequent, and safe deployments
- Minimize downtime and improve system reliability
- Automate manual processes and reduce toil
- Maintain security and compliance standards

### Typical Communication
- Infrastructure and deployment documentation
- Incident reports and post-mortems
- Pipeline status updates and release coordination
- Security scanning results and remediation plans

### Interactions with Core Roles
- **Developers**: Provide tooling and automation for build, test, and deployment; support debugging production issues
- **Project Managers**: Communicate deployment schedules and infrastructure dependencies; flag technical risks
- **QA Lead**: Coordinate test environment provisioning and deployment automation
- **Product Managers**: Ensure infrastructure supports product scalability and reliability goals

---

## QA Lead

### Role Summary
QA Leads ensure product quality through comprehensive testing strategies, quality processes, and team coordination. They balance speed and quality to deliver reliable software that meets user expectations.

### Responsibilities
- Define testing strategies and quality standards
- Coordinate test planning across functional, integration, and end-to-end testing
- Review test coverage and identify quality gaps
- Lead QA team and coordinate testing activities
- Automate repetitive testing tasks
- Track and report on quality metrics and defect trends

### Goals
- Deliver high-quality releases with minimal defects
- Reduce time to detect and resolve quality issues
- Improve test coverage and automation
- Ensure features meet acceptance criteria before release

### Typical Communication
- Test plans and testing schedules
- Quality reports and defect metrics
- Test case documentation and coverage analysis
- Release readiness assessments

### Interactions with Core Roles
- **Developers**: Coordinate bug fixes and provide reproduction steps; collaborate on test automation
- **Product Managers**: Validate acceptance criteria and feature quality; provide quality insights for prioritization
- **Project Managers**: Report testing progress and quality risks; coordinate release readiness
- **DevOps Engineer**: Collaborate on test environment setup and CI integration
- **UX Designer**: Validate user experience against design specifications

---

## Stakeholder Representative

### Role Summary
Stakeholder Representatives voice the needs and constraints of key business groups (e.g., sales, support, compliance, executive leadership). They ensure project decisions consider broader organizational impact and alignment.

### Responsibilities
- Represent stakeholder interests in project planning and decisions
- Communicate project updates to their stakeholder groups
- Provide domain expertise and business context
- Review deliverables from stakeholder perspective
- Escalate concerns and facilitate stakeholder alignment
- Support change management and adoption in their organization

### Goals
- Ensure project outcomes serve stakeholder needs
- Maintain alignment between project and organizational strategy
- Facilitate smooth adoption and change management
- Minimize negative impact on stakeholder operations

### Typical Communication
- Stakeholder feedback and requirements input
- Impact assessments and concerns
- Change readiness reports
- Adoption metrics and user feedback from their domain

### Interactions with Core Roles
- **Product Managers**: Provide stakeholder input on priorities and requirements; validate product direction
- **Project Managers**: Communicate stakeholder expectations and constraints; participate in governance and decisions
- **Business Analyst**: Provide domain expertise and validate requirements from stakeholder perspective
- **Developers**: Offer business context for technical decisions

## Role Onboarding Checklist

This checklist helps new team members understand their responsibilities and integration points with other roles.

### For All Roles
- [ ] Review the OctoAcme Project Management Overview
- [ ] Understand the project's goals, success metrics, and timeline
- [ ] Identify your primary points of contact for each core role (PM, PdM, Dev Lead)
- [ ] Review the project's communication channels and meeting cadence
- [ ] Access project boards, repositories, and documentation
- [ ] Understand the project's risk register and current blockers

### Role-Specific Onboarding

#### UX Designer
- [ ] Review existing design system and style guides
- [ ] Understand target users and any existing research
- [ ] Set up design tools and access to prototyping platforms
- [ ] Schedule design review sessions with PM and engineering
- [ ] Identify accessibility and usability standards to follow

#### Business Analyst
- [ ] Review existing requirements documentation and specifications
- [ ] Meet with key stakeholders to understand business context
- [ ] Understand current business processes and pain points
- [ ] Set up access to business analysis tools and templates
- [ ] Review Definition of Done and acceptance criteria standards

#### DevOps Engineer
- [ ] Get access to cloud infrastructure and deployment platforms
- [ ] Review existing CI/CD pipelines and infrastructure as code
- [ ] Understand deployment processes and rollback procedures
- [ ] Review monitoring, logging, and alerting setup
- [ ] Understand security and compliance requirements

#### QA Lead
- [ ] Review test strategy and existing test coverage
- [ ] Access test management tools and test environments
- [ ] Understand automation framework and testing standards
- [ ] Review Definition of Done and quality gates
- [ ] Coordinate with development team on testing approach

#### Stakeholder Representative
- [ ] Understand which stakeholder group(s) you represent
- [ ] Identify key concerns and success criteria for your stakeholders
- [ ] Establish communication cadence with project leadership
- [ ] Review change management and adoption plans
- [ ] Understand decision-making and escalation processes
